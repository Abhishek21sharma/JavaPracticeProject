9. "How do you handle a developer who says 'It works on my machine' when you find a bug?"
This shows your Communication and Mentorship skills. Focus on collaboration, not confrontation.
"I view this as an opportunity to align our environments. Rather than a 'back-and-forth,' I provide a clear, reproducible report—ideally with a Playwright trace file or a containerized environment link. I’ll often sit down with the developer to compare the CI/CD environment logs with their local setup. Usually, it’s a configuration or data seeding issue. By approaching it as a 'joint investigation' rather than a 'critique,' I build the trust necessary to maintain a high-performing culture where quality is a shared responsibility."
10. "How do you decide what to automate and what to keep manual?"
Use a framework, ention the Test Automation Pyramid.
I prioritize automation for high-risk, high-frequency, and stable features.
11. "Tell me about a time you had to handle a high-priority defect found right before a release."
We focus on : Focus on Root Cause Analysis (RCA) and Process Improvement.
"In a previous role, we identified a critical integration bug during the final regression suite for a major release. My immediate priority was a Risk Assessment: determining if we could patch, feature-flag the issue, or if a 'Go/No-Go' decision was necessary. After the immediate fix, I led a root cause analysis. We discovered a gap in our API contract testing. I then implemented a 'Shift-Left' strategy by introducing contract tests in the pull-request stage, ensuring that specific type of failure could never reach the release branch again. This turned a one-time crisis into a permanent increase in our deployment frequency and reliability."
